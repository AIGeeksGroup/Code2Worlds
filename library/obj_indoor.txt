1. z_cup must be less than z_length, z_mid must be greater than z_cup and less than z_length.
2. x_top is usually greater than x_end, x_mid may be greater or less than x_top (depending on log_uniform(0.9, 1.2) result).
3. x_neck is the smallest radius value, used to form the neck.
4. Wine glass shape is defined through x_anchors and z_anchors, these anchor points are auto-calculated from above parameters.
5. Material parameters are auto-generated via weighted_sample, usually no manual setting needed.

Example Combinations (Semantic to Parameters)
- "A medium-height wine glass, thin neck, narrow opening, obvious waist, thin wall":
  z_length=1.0, scale=0.2, x_end=0.25, z_cup=0.45*z_length, z_mid=z_cup+0.4*(z_length-z_cup), x_neck=0.012, x_top=x_end*1.15, x_mid=x_top*0.95, thickness=0.015

- "A tall wine glass, thick neck, wide opening, cylindrical, thick wall":
  z_length=1.6, scale=0.25, x_end=0.25, z_cup=0.55*z_length, z_mid=z_cup+0.45*(z_length-z_cup), x_neck=0.018, x_top=x_end*1.3, x_mid=x_top*1.1, thickness=0.025

- "A short wine glass, medium neck, medium opening, slight waist, medium wall thickness":
